Marvel is a leading entertainment company known for its comic books, films, and television shows. It was founded in 1939 by Martin Goodman as Timely Publications and later became known as Atlas Comics before adopting the name Marvel in the 1960s. 

Marvel is famous for creating iconic superheroes such as Spider-Man, Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, Hulk, Black Widow, and the X-Men. Many of these characters have gained global popularity and have been adapted into successful films. 

The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) was launched in 2008 with the release of "Iron Man." It is a shared universe where multiple Marvel superheroes coexist and cross paths in various films and TV shows. The MCU has proven to be incredibly successful, with films like "Avengers: Endgame" becoming some of the highest-grossing movies of all time.

In addition to the MCU, Marvel has produced a range of TV shows, including "Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.," "Daredevil," "Jessica Jones," "Luke Cage," "Iron Fist," and "The Punisher." These shows expand the Marvel universe by exploring different characters and storylines.

Marvel Comics, the publishing division of Marvel, releases various comic book series featuring its beloved characters. Notable comics include "The Amazing Spider-Man," "The Avengers," "X-Men," "Fantastic Four," and "Black Panther."

Over the years, Marvel has built a vast and interconnected universe across different platforms, which has contributed to its immense popularity and dedicated fan base. It continues to produce new content and expand its universe through movies, TV shows, and comic books.
